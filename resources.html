Websites

NeuroClastic

NeuroClastic is an educational website that is run by Autistic advocates, and which publishes articles written almost exclusively by Autistic contributors. Check out their mission statement, and read more about their commitment to amplifying Autistic voices, here.



Books

Divergent Mind

This book written by Jenara Nerenberg provides incredible insight into the experiences of Autistic individuals, with a particular focus drawn on Autistic women. An Autistic self-advocate, Nerenberg references the difficulties she has faced in navigating the world through a neurodivergent lens, with emphasis placed on the ways in which her identity as a woman has impacted her efforts to be respected and supported within neurotypical society. Click here to visit Nerenberg's website, where you can learn more about her work with The Neurodiversity Project.


Extremely Loud and Incredibly Close

This novel written by Jonathan Safran Foer explores the experiences of Oskar Schell, a nine-year-old boy who is attempting to navigate life following the death of his father during the 9/11 attacks. While several mental health themes are touched upon throughout the book, Oskar's character provides excellent insight into the Autistic experience, given that many self-advocates believe him to be on the spectrum. Click here to read more about the book on Wikipedia.


NeuroTribes

This book written by Steve Silberman brings to light both the history and the potential future of autism, with a focus on the importance of embracing neurodiversity. An Autistic self-advocate, Silberman offers a refreshing perspective that paints ASD as something that exists naturally, beyond the stigmatizing medical model. Many consider Silberman's book instrumental in bringing power and advocacy to the neurodivergent community. Click here to read more about the book on Wikipedia.


On the Edge of Gone

This novel written by Corinne Duyvis features a Black Autistic protagonist named Denise, who is trying to find her place in an apocolyptic-era society. Many themes relating to mental health are explored in the book, and it is worth noting that Denise's Autistic identity is not only confirmed, but is also referenced openly and extensively throughout the story. Duyvis's novel also offers a wealth of LGBTQ+ representation. Click here to read a review of the book by Ada Hoffmann, an Autistic self-advocate who works with Disability in Kidlit to critique the portrayal of disability in literature.



Movies

Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them

This film features a character named Newt Scamander, who is widely believed to be Autistic. Many Autistic fans of the movie series feel a strong connection to Newt, and the actor who plays him, Eddie Redmayne, has even spoken about his convinction that the character falls somewhere on the autism spectrum. Click here to read more about the film on Wikipedia.


The Reason I Jump

This documentary, which is based on the book written by Autistic self-advocate Naoki Higashida, provides intimate insight into the rich lives of Autistic individuals through a variety of immersive sensory experiences. With a focus on five non-speaking Autistic young adults from different cultural and geographic backgrounds, the film encourages its viewers to view autism, and neurodivergence, through a more informed and empowered lens. Click here to visit the documentary's designated website, where you can watch the trailer and learn more about the makings of the film.
